The team

The people behind the path

Few — but with a big heart and a single mission: to connect remembrance in Israel with Jewish communities around the world.

Team members

CEO

Nerya Meir

Attorney Zionist entrepreneur Public activist Chairman of World Betar (former) Head of the Department for Zionist Activity in the Diaspora (former)

Nerya Meir leads "Bishvileinu" out of a deep belief in the power of memory to connect Israel with Jewish communities around the world. For many years he has worked to strengthen the bond with Diaspora Jewry — as Chairman of World Betar (2013–2021), during which new strongholds of the movement were established in France, Brazil, Ukraine and more — and later as Head of the Department for Zionist Activity in the Diaspora at the World Zionist Organization.

An attorney by training and holder of a master's degree in public policy, Nerya founded the "Western Wall Company" (Plugat HaKotel) museum in Jerusalem, and acts out of a constant sense of mission to strengthen the identity, resilience and mutual responsibility of the Jewish people.

During the Swords of Iron War, Nerya went to fight as a reserve soldier in Brigade 55, the Paratroopers Brigade — from that same place of giving and responsibility from which "Bishvileinu" grew.

Nerya is married and a father of five, lives in Rechelim, and in 2014 donated a kidney to his brother — all of which is an ongoing act of giving.

Digital Manager

Shilo Meir

Website Social media Content & storytelling

Shilo Meir leads the digital presence of "Bishvileinu" — from the website to social media. He is responsible for telling the stories of remembrance and growth in a contemporary digital language, and for building the online bridges that connect communities worldwide with the initiative. Believing that every story deserves to travel far, Shilo makes sure the message of "Bishvileinu" reaches every home and every heart.
